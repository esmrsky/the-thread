## 1. Paul Ellis & Escape to Reality — Core Framework

**Bio:** Dr. Paul Ellis is a former Bible smuggler, pastor, and business-school professor. He pastored a multicultural church in Hong Kong for ten years, has lived in Australia, Hong Kong, and the US, and now lives in New Zealand with his wife Camilla and their four children. He describes hitting a wall of burnout and legalism in ministry before "discovering" grace as a Person (Jesus) rather than a doctrine — this personal crash-and-recovery story is the origin point he cites for his whole message.
Source: [Bio for Paul Ellis](https://escapetoreality.org/about-2/bio-for-paul-ellis/), [About Dr. Paul Ellis](https://escapetoreality.org/about/)

**Site:** Escape to Reality (E2R) launched February 5, 2010. It now holds roughly 1,000+ articles indexed by scripture and subject, reaches readers in 200+ countries, and Ellis calls it (per reader feedback) "the Wikipedia of Grace." His books have been translated into nearly a dozen languages.
Source: [escapetoreality.org](https://escapetoreality.org/)

**Core framework (the throughline across everything he writes):**
- Grace is a Person (Jesus), not a doctrine or a force.
- Hard covenant line: the Old Covenant (law, "we will" promises humans kept breaking) is obsolete; the New Covenant (grace, "he will" promises God alone keeps) is what believers live under. Mixing the two ("mixture" / being a "mixaholic") is, in his view, the single biggest cause of Christian defeat and joylessness.
- Identity over behavior: believers are already loved, forgiven, righteous, holy, and secure *in Christ* — not on probation, not earning standing through performance.
- The cross is a finished, one-time, fully sufficient event (Hebrews 9–10) — nothing is added to it by human effort, confession-of-every-sin, or ongoing sacrifice.
- Consistently reads Scripture "through Jesus" — if an interpretation makes God look like Jesus never revealed Him (angry, transactional, score-keeping), Ellis treats that as a signal of misreading, not a fact about God.

## 2. Paul Ellis's Books

### The Gospel in Ten Words (2012)
**The verified ten words** (confirmed across three independent sources — the original E2R article, an independent book-review blog, and cross-checked search summaries): **Loved, Forgiven, Saved, Union, Accepted, Holy, Righteous, Died (dead to sin), New, Royal.** The book's own framing sentence is: *"In union with Christ you are loved, forgiven, saved, accepted, holy, righteous, dead to sin, new, and royal."* Each word gets its own short chapter, and Ellis is explicit that these are **not** sequential steps or levels of maturity — "pearls on a necklace," equally true of every believer, no exceptions.
Sources: [The Gospel in Ten Words](https://escapetoreality.org/2012/09/17/the-gospel-in-ten-words/), [10th Anniversary post](https://escapetoreality.org/2022/09/15/the-gospel-in-ten-words-tenth-anniversary/), [independent review confirming the list](https://dotheword.org/2013/07/01/the-gospel-in-ten-words-try-pauls/), [Amazon listing](https://www.amazon.com/Gospel-Ten-Words-Paul-Ellis/dp/1927230004)

### The Gospel in Twenty Questions (2013)
Answers 20 questions believers commonly wrestle with while trying to live by grace — opening with "Who's your daddy?" and including "Does God make us sick to teach us things?", "What is the unforgivable sin?", and "Once saved, always saved?" Winner of a Christian Small Publishers Book of the Year award.
Source: [Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/Gospel-Twenty-Questions-Paul-Ellis/dp/1927230101), [review](https://www.stevehackman.net/paul-ellis-and-the-gospel-in-twenty-questions/)

### The Hyper-Grace Gospel (2014)
A direct response to charismatic apologist Michael Brown's book *Hyper-Grace: Exposing the Dangers of the Modern Grace Message* (Jan 2014) — Ellis's response came out that April. First half declares the "Jesus plus nothing" gospel; second half is a chapter-by-chapter rebuttal of specific objections, drawing on 40+ grace preachers and including a "12 Myths About the Hyper-Grace Gospel" section (see Section 3 below). Won a Silver Medal in the Illumination Book Awards.
Source: [Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/Hyper-Grace-Gospel-Response-Michael-Opposed/dp/1927230152), [Michael Brown controversy background](https://escapetoreality.org/2016/05/04/michael-brown-grace-controversy/), [Confronting the Error of Hyper-Grace response](https://escapetoreality.org/2013/02/22/error-of-hyper-grace-michael-brown/)

### Stuff Jesus Never Said (2016)
Contrasts what Jesus actually said (on divorce, guilt, depression, giving, etc.) with what popular religious culture assumes He said, arguing that 2,000 years of "manmade religion" has left many Christians more familiar with words Jesus never spoke than His actual words. Illustrated with classic religious art. Gold Medal, 2017 Illumination Book Awards; Book of the Year, 2017 CSPA Awards.
Source: [Out Today announcement](https://escapetoreality.org/2016/08/24/stuff-jesus-never-said/), [Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/Stuff-Jesus-Never-Said-Ellis/dp/1927230365)

### Letters from Jesus (2018)
Walks through Christ's seven letters to the churches in Revelation 2–3, arguing these are among the most misread passages in the Bible — read as threats when Ellis says they're good news start to finish. Addresses head-on: Does God punish me when I sin? Will Jesus erase my name from the book of life? Am I lukewarm? 2020 Christian Indie Award winner.
Source: [Google Books](https://books.google.com/books/about/Letters_from_Jesus.html?id=22kHEAAAQBAJ), [The Gospel in the Seven Letters](https://escapetoreality.org/2019/04/25/gospel-in-the-seven-letters/)

### AD70 and the End of the World (2017)
An eschatology book reframing Jesus's judgment prophecies (Olivet Discourse, etc.) and the fall of Jerusalem in AD 70 as good news rather than fear-fuel. Structured partly as a dialogue between "the preterist" and "the futurist." Notable as reportedly the only end-times book endorsed by preterists *and* futurists alike, since Ellis intentionally avoids picking a side on timing while still de-fanging fear-based end-times preaching.
Source: [Out Today announcement](https://escapetoreality.org/2017/04/11/ad70-and-the-end-of-the-world-out-today/), [Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/AD70-End-World-Prophecies-Parables/dp/1927230411)

### The Grace Commentary (gracecommentary.com / thegracecommentary.com)
A free, ongoing, verse-by-verse online Bible commentary launched March 2021 — explicitly grace-based, deliberately excludes condemnation/guilt-driven notes, and draws a hard line between law and grace and old/new covenants on every page. Being compiled into a print edition called **The Grace Bible**, aimed at putting a "mixture-free" study Bible in the hands of everyday believers. As of recent posts it's read in 170+ countries.
Source: [thegracecommentary.com](https://thegracecommentary.com/), [Introducing the Grace Commentary](https://escapetoreality.org/2021/03/23/introducing-the-grace-commentary/), [thegracebible.com](https://thegracebible.com/about/)

## 3. Best Recurring Article Themes

### 1 John 1:9 and confession
Ellis's most-argued single verse. His claim: 1 John 1:9 ("If we confess our sins, he is faithful and just to forgive us...") is addressed to people **not yet walking in fellowship/light** — he reads it against the backdrop of early gnostic-style claims of sinlessness, not as an ongoing formula believers must repeat to stay forgiven. He argues *confess* (Gk. *homologeo*) means "to agree with" or "say the same thing as," i.e., agreeing with God about your condition and receiving forgiveness *already secured* at the cross — not earning fresh forgiveness sin-by-sin. **[DISPUTED]** — his companion piece goes further, arguing 1 John 1 itself may be addressed to unconverted/mixed audiences rather than to believers; this is a minority reading most commentators don't hold (most read 1 John 1 as written to genuine believers).
- Key verses: 1 John 1:9, 1 John 2:2 ("he is the atoning sacrifice... not for ours only but also for the sins of the whole world"), Hebrews 10:14, Hebrews 9:26
- Sources: [1 John 1:9 – Something Old, Something New](https://escapetoreality.org/2012/06/08/1-john-1v9/), [1 John 1:9 – Ask the Right Question](https://escapetoreality.org/2014/06/03/1-john-1v9-the-right-question/), [Is 1 John 1 for Believers or Unbelievers?](https://escapetoreality.org/2012/06/14/is-1-john-1-for-believers/), [Are Grace Preachers Against Confession?](https://escapetoreality.org/2014/09/04/myth-post-2-hyper-grace-preachers-are-against-confession/)

### Repentance as change of mind (metanoia)
Ellis doesn't reject repentance — he redefines it. *Metanoia* = "change of mind," not "turn from sin" through willpower/moral effort. His 3 stated reasons for not preaching "repent = turn from sin": (1) it re-enthrones law-keeping and actually empowers the sin it condemns; (2) it points people the wrong direction (fleeing sin) instead of the right one (turning **to** God/trusting the gospel); (3) the apostolic pattern (e.g., Paul at Corinth) was to preach Christ crucified, with repentance following as a *response* to God's kindness, not a precondition for it.
- Key verses: Mark 1:15, Romans 2:4 ("God's kindness leads you toward repentance"), Romans 1:17, 1 Corinthians 2:2
- Sources: [3 Reasons Why I Don't Preach on Repentance](https://escapetoreality.org/2011/11/28/3-reasons-why-i-dont-preach-on-repentance/), [Are Hypergrace Preachers Opposed to Repentance?](https://escapetoreality.org/2014/08/20/hyper-grace-repentance/), [Why repentance is like football](https://escapetoreality.org/2011/11/20/why-repentance-is-like-football/)

### The prodigal son / sons vs. servants
Ellis's signature identity article isn't a straight retelling — it's "Son, Servant, or Friend of God?", arguing most Christians (like the elder brother in Luke 15) relate to God through obligation and performance rather than secure sonship, which he calls an "orphan spirit." Neither son in the parable actually trusted the father's love — the younger ran from it, the elder worked for it without ever enjoying it. Function (serving) should flow *from* identity (belovedness), never the reverse.
- Key verses: Luke 15:11-32, Romans 8:15 ("Spirit of sonship... Abba, Father"), Galatians 4:6, Romans 8:17, John 15:14-15
- Source: [Son, Servant, or Friend of God?](https://escapetoreality.org/2010/10/04/son-servant-or-friend-of-god/)

### Mixing old and new covenants
Central Ellis theme: the old covenant ran on human promises ("we will do all the Lord has said," Exodus 19:8) that were broken repeatedly; the new covenant runs on God's unbreakable promises ("I will," Jeremiah 32:40) — summarized as "we will" vs. "he will." He calls himself "a recovering mixaholic" and treats Galatians as the definitive warning against blending law and grace. Under grace, blessing flows "because he is good," not because of performance.
- Key verses: Jeremiah 32:40, Hebrews 8:13 ("obsolete... will soon disappear"), Galatians throughout (esp. 3:1-3), Exodus 19:8, Romans 4:8
- Sources: [The Old vs the New Covenant (Jeremiah 32:40)](https://escapetoreality.org/2024/02/22/the-old-vs-the-new-covenant/), [Old vs. new](https://escapetoreality.org/2011/10/11/old-vs-new/), [Is "Love your Neighbor" Part of the New Covenant?](https://escapetoreality.org/2024/10/24/is-love-your-neighbor-part-of-the-new-covenant/)

6. Hyper-grace preachers are against the law
7. Hyper-grace preachers ignore the Old Testament
8. Hyper-grace preachers disregard the words of Jesus

10. The hyper-grace gospel discourages obedience and holy living

- Sources: [Who Are the Hypergrace Preachers?](https://escapetoreality.org/2016/09/22/who-are-the-hypergrace-preachers/), [Myth: Hypergrace Preachers Disregard the Words of Jesus](https://escapetoreality.org/2015/03/04/disregarding-the-words-of-jesus/), [Does the Hypergrace Gospel Encourage Sin? (Myth 9)](https://escapetoreality.org/2017/03/16/myth-9-the-hyper-grace-gospel-encourages-sin/), [hyper-grace category index](https://escapetoreality.org/category/hyper-grace/)

### Does grace lead to license?
Answered head-on in multiple articles: grace is "not a license to sin" — true grace *trains* believers toward godliness (Titus 2:11-12) and severs sin's power rather than excusing it (Romans 6:1-2, 5:20). Ellis's line: those who weaponize grace as an excuse either misunderstand their new identity or were never trusting Christ to begin with; law produces "dead works" and exhaustion, while grace produces good works that "change the world." A companion piece tackles the "grace makes you lazy" version of the same objection.
- Key verses: Romans 6:1-2, Romans 5:20, Titus 2:11-12
- Sources: [Is Grace a License to Sin?](https://escapetoreality.org/2012/09/24/is-grace-a-license-to-sin/), [Is Grace a License to be Lazy?](https://escapetoreality.org/2016/06/15/is-grace-a-license-to-be-lazy/), [Does the Hypergrace Gospel Encourage Sin?](https://escapetoreality.org/2017/03/16/myth-9-the-hyper-grace-gospel-encourages-sin/)

### Tithing
Multi-part series concluding tithing is not a New Covenant requirement. Reads Genesis 14 (Abram's tithe to Melchizedek, pictured as Jesus) as spontaneous generosity in response to grace, contrasted with the King of Sodom's offer (the flesh/law). "Should you tithe?" line: *"in Christ Jesus neither tithing nor non-tithing has any value. If you want to tithe, then tithe, and if you don't, then don't."* Notes Paul never once commands tithing in his letters, and warns against using Malachi 3 ("robbing God") to guilt believers into giving.
- Key verses: Genesis 14:17-23, Genesis 28:22, Malachi 3:8-10, 2 Corinthians 9:6-7, Matthew 23:23, Luke 19:8
- Sources: [Tithing Under Grace](https://escapetoreality.org/2016/07/15/tithing-under-grace/), [Should you Tithe?](https://escapetoreality.org/2016/07/19/should-you-tithe/), [Jesus is Your Tithe](https://escapetoreality.org/2017/09/07/jesus-is-your-tithe/), [Jesus our Tithe](https://escapetoreality.org/2016/07/21/jesus-our-tithe/), [He Who Sows Sparingly (2 Cor 9:6)](https://escapetoreality.org/2021/05/20/he-who-sows-sparingly/)

### God's discipline
Ellis's most provocative reframe: "Does God Scourge Us?" argues **no** — Hebrews 12:6's "scourgeth" (KJV) describes a punishment historically reserved for slaves/criminals, never sons, and Ellis reads it as describing persecution believers *endure* (context: a suffering, persecuted audience) rather than pain God *inflicts*. He argues Jesus never once afflicted anyone for correction, and that biblical fathering means "nurture and admonition" (Ephesians 6:4), not affliction. **[DISPUTED]** — this is a minority reading; the plain-sense majority reading of Hebrews 12:5-11 (including most conservative commentaries) takes divine "discipline/chastening" of believers, including hard providence, as a real and loving category, not solely persecution-endurance. Worth presenting on the site as "Ellis's contested take," not settled consensus.
- Key verses: Hebrews 12:5-11 (esp. v.6), Isaiah 53:5, Ephesians 6:4, Proverbs 26:3
- Source: [Does God Scourge Us? 7 Reasons to Say "No!"](https://escapetoreality.org/2013/06/18/does-god-scourge-us/)

### Eternal security
One of Ellis's biggest resource investments: he says he personally read every scripture (~300) that could bear on losing salvation and found roughly half seem to warn against it and half seem to affirm security — but "reading them through Jesus-glasses," he concludes none actually teaches a true child of God can be "unchilded." He has crowdsourced/compiled a list of 130+ scriptures supporting eternal security.
- Key verses: John 10:28-29, Romans 8:38-39, Ephesians 1:13-14, Philippians 1:6, 2 Timothy 2:13
- Sources: [Once Saved, Always Saved?](https://escapetoreality.org/2024/09/12/once-saved-always-saved/), [The Top 12 Promises on Eternal Security](https://escapetoreality.org/2017/06/08/12-promises-on-eternal-security/), [Eternal Security Scriptures (resource page)](https://escapetoreality.org/resources/eternal-security/), [Can You Lose Your Salvation?](https://escapetoreality.org/2018/02/01/can-you-lose-your-salvation/), [Does 2 Peter 2 Say You Can Lose Your Salvation?](https://escapetoreality.org/2017/05/11/2-peter-2/)

## 4. Healing Theology

### Key verses (verified exact text, NIV unless noted)
- **Isaiah 53:4-5 (KJV, as most commonly quoted in healing contexts):** *"Surely he hath borne our griefs, and carried our sorrows... But he was wounded for our transgressions, he was bruised for our iniquities: the chastisement of our peace was upon him; and with his stripes we are healed."*
- **Matthew 8:16-17 (NIV):** *"When evening came, many who were demon-possessed were brought to him, and he drove out the spirits with a word and healed all the sick. This was to fulfill what was spoken through the prophet Isaiah: 'He took up our infirmities and bore our diseases.'"*
- **1 Peter 2:24 (KJV, common healing-context translation):** *"Who his own self bare our sins in his own body on the tree, that we, being dead to sins, should live unto righteousness: by whose stripes ye were healed."*
- **Acts 10:38 (NIV):** *"how God anointed Jesus of Nazareth with the Holy Spirit and power, and how he went around doing good and healing all who were under the power of the devil, because God was with him."*
- **Matthew 4:23-24 (NIV, paraphrase-verified):** Jesus went through Galilee "healing every disease and sickness among the people," and "they brought to him all who were ill... and he healed them."
- **Matthew 9:35 (NIV):** *"Jesus went through all the towns and villages, teaching in their synagogues, proclaiming the good news of the kingdom and healing every disease and sickness."*
- **Matthew 12:15 (ESV):** *"...and many followed him, and he healed them all."*
- **Luke 6:19 (paraphrase-verified):** *"The entire crowd was trying to touch him, because power was coming from him and healing them all."*

**The core theological move** (shared across Ellis, Wommack, Bosworth, and the wider healing-affirming camp): Matthew 8:16-17 explicitly cites Isaiah 53 and applies it to *physical* healing in Jesus's earthly ministry, not merely spiritual/emotional healing — so healing advocates argue sickness, like sin, was dealt with at the cross. This is a real, live debate in evangelical scholarship, not just grace-camp vs. everyone else: mainstream cessationist and continuationist scholars alike often hold healing is *secured* in the atonement but not *guaranteed in full* until resurrection/glorification (an "already/not yet" framing), while healing-ministry teachers (Ellis, Wommack, Bosworth) tend to argue it should be treated as fully available *now*, the same way forgiveness is. **[DISPUTED — flag as a live theological debate, not settled either direction.]**
Sources: [The Gospel Coalition: Does Matthew 8 Teach Physical Healing in the Atonement?](https://www.thegospelcoalition.org/article/physical-healing-atonement/), [Bodily Healing and the Atonement by T.J. McCrossan (recommended by Ellis)](https://escapetoreality.org/2011/01/20/%E2%80%9Cbodily-healing-and-the-atonement%E2%80%9D-by-tj-mccrossan/)

### Standard objections, answered by grace/healing teachers

**"Paul's thorn in the flesh was sickness, so not everyone gets healed" (2 Corinthians 12:7-10).** Wommack and other healing teachers argue the thorn was a **"messenger of Satan"** — read as ongoing demonic opposition/persecution (pointing to the list of afflictions in 2 Cor 11:23-29: imprisonments, beatings, shipwrecks) — not a physical illness. **[DISPUTED — genuinely, among scholars generally, not just grace-camp vs. traditional.]** Mainstream commentators are actually split several ways: some favor a physical ailment (a popular theory is chronic eye disease, drawing on Galatians 4:13-15 where Paul mentions an illness and the Galatians' willingness to "tear out their eyes" for him), others favor persecution/opposition, others temptation. It's fair to describe this as unresolved rather than settled in the grace camp's favor.
Sources: [Andrew Wommack: Paul's Thorn in the Flesh](https://www.awmi.net/browse-teaching/pauls-thorn-in-the-flesh/), [Crossway: What Was Paul's Thorn in the Flesh?](https://www.crossway.org/articles/what-was-pauls-thorn-in-the-flesh-2-corinthians-12/)

**"Timothy had a bad stomach and Paul told him to drink wine, not just believe for healing" (1 Timothy 5:23).** Healing teachers' standard answer: Paul giving Timothy practical medical advice ("a little wine for your stomach") is not a contradiction of divine healing — it shows Paul was comfortable with ordinary means alongside faith, not that healing is off the table. Sometimes framed as "God expects us to use wisdom for some things and the supernatural for others" — Paul, who raised the dead, didn't treat every ailment supernaturally.
Source: [1 Timothy 5:23 discussion](https://www.bibleref.com/1-Timothy/5/1-Timothy-5-23.html)

**"Job proves God sometimes sends sickness to teach us."** Grace/healing answer: Scripture is explicit that Job's affliction was Satan's doing, done *with God's permission* (Job 2:3-7), not God's initiative — and it was temporary, reversed within the story. Healing teachers often add a covenant-timing argument: Job's story predates the cross and predates Satan's decisive defeat there (Colossians 2:15), so it describes an access-to-accuse arrangement (cf. Job 1:6-12) that they argue no longer applies the same way post-resurrection. **[Note: the covenant-timing argument is a theological inference, not something the text states directly — worth flagging as an interpretive move, not a stated fact.]**
Sources: [Job's Healing: Did God Really Cause His Suffering?](https://graceforhealing.com/jobs-healing-god-responsible/), Job 2:3-7, Job 1:6-12, Colossians 2:15

**"God is teaching me through this sickness / it's for my sanctification."** Grace teachers' answer, consolidated from Ellis's "9 Lies of Religion": this reverses Jesus's own pattern — He never once made anyone sick to teach them a lesson; He took sickness *away* everywhere He found it (see the "healed them all" passages above). Ellis: *"God NEVER gives us sickness to teach us things."* He also engages the hard test case of **Exodus 4:11** ("Who makes him deaf or mute or seeing or blind? Is it not I, the LORD?") — arguing the verse describes God as Creator/sovereign over all people (including those born with conditions), not as the active giver of individual disabilities, and points to Jesus (the "exact imprint" of the Father, Hebrews 1:3) always healing, never afflicting, as the interpretive key.
Sources: [The 9 Lies of Religion](https://escapetoreality.org/2015/04/01/the-9-lies-of-religion/), [Exodus 4:11 article](https://escapetoreality.org/2010/08/13/exodus-411/), [Does God Give Bad Gifts?](https://escapetoreality.org/2010/07/26/does-god-give-bad-gifts/)

### Communion and healing (1 Corinthians 11:29-30)
- **1 Corinthians 11:29 (NIV):** *"For anyone who eats and drinks without discerning the body of Christ eats and drinks judgment on themselves."*
- **1 Corinthians 11:30 (NIV):** *"That is why many among you are weak and sick, and a number of you have fallen asleep."*

Two live readings: (1) **Traditional/majority view** — Paul's rebuke is about the Corinthians' careless, divisive conduct at the shared meal (richer members gorging while poorer members went hungry, failing to "discern" the church as Christ's body — a relational/ethical failure), and the resulting weakness/sickness is presented as a form of God's corrective judgment on that specific misconduct. (2) **Healing-camp reading (including Ellis)** — communion, taken with understanding of what Christ's broken body accomplished (including physical healing per Isaiah 53/Matthew 8), is itself a vehicle of healing; sickness in verse 30 results from *failing to discern/appropriate* the healing already purchased, not from a punitive judgment as such. Present both readings; this is genuinely contested among commentators, not just a grace-vs-tradition split.
Source: [Escape to Reality: 1 Corinthians 11:26-32](https://escapetoreality.org/2011/04/12/1-corinthians-1126-32/), [TGC Africa: How to Discern the Body](https://africa.thegospelcoalition.org/article/how-to-discern-the-body-a-brief-study-of-1-corinthians-11/)

### How grace/healing teachers frame medicine
Consistent across every mainstream healing teacher researched (Ellis, Wommack, Bosworth successors): **not anti-doctor.** The framing is "God heals through natural means and supernatural means — both are His" rather than an either/or. 1 Timothy 5:23 (Paul's wine-for-the-stomach advice to Timothy) is regularly cited as proof Paul himself endorsed practical/medical means alongside faith. The distinctly "faith-only, refuse-all-medicine" position exists at the fringes of the wider faith-healing world but is **not** the position of the teachers in this research set — worth being precise about that distinction on the site so readers don't conflate "healing is available" with "never see a doctor."
Sources: [Medicine and divine healing – IAHM](https://healing-ministries.org/medicine-and-divine-healing/), [1 Timothy 5:23 discussion](https://www.bibleref.com/1-Timothy/5/1-Timothy-5-23.html)

### Two healing books Ellis specifically recommends
"Divine Healing Made Simple" by Praying Medic (praised as practical, written by an actual paramedic) and "Healing the Sick" by T.L. Osborn (a healing-ministry classic, 1M+ copies printed, heavy on documented testimony).
Source: [Two books on healing](https://escapetoreality.org/2015/04/15/good-books-on-healing/)

## 5. Adjacent Voices

**Andrew Farley** — Pastor of The Grace Church (Lubbock, TX), professor, and host of the long-running radio/podcast/YouTube ministry *The Grace Message*. Best entry point: **"The Naked Gospel: The Truth You May Never Hear in Church"** — Farley's personal-burnout-to-freedom narrative (strikingly similar arc to Ellis's own story) arguing that mixing Old and New Covenant obligations is the root cause of Christian defeat, and that believers are entirely free from the Law, including its role as a behavior-monitor for sanctification. His ministry reports 7M+ podcast downloads and reaches large SiriusXM/TBN audiences. *Caution:* Farley is explicitly identified with the "hyper-grace" label and draws the standard critique that eliminating the Law's ongoing role could, in critics' view, undercut accountability — Farley's standing reply is that real grace transforms rather than licenses, and that fear of sin never produced lasting holiness anyway. Good entry-point video/audio: his *"9 Nuances of the New Covenant"* and *"The Message"* teachings.
Sources: [andrewfarley.org](https://andrewfarley.org/), [Naked Gospel summary](https://morgantrotter.wordpress.com/2015/06/17/the-naked-gospel-by-andrew-farley-summary-and-response/), [criticism roundup](https://standforthefaith.com/2017/09/21/beware-of-hyper-grace-andrew-farely-and-jeremy-white/)

**Andrew Wommack** — Founder of Andrew Wommack Ministries and Charis Bible College; one of the most prolific healing/grace teachers alive, with a teaching archive spanning decades. Best entry points: **"The True Nature of God"** (contrasts Old Covenant severity with New Covenant grace, aiming to rebuild trust in God's character) and **"God Wants You Well"** (argues healing is as fully secured in the atonement as forgiveness, and directly tackles the "thorn in the flesh" objection). *Caution — this one is a real, documented concern worth naming plainly:* a Gospel Coalition/Themelios review by Stephen Witmer raises four substantive critiques — an "over-realized eschatology" that treats future resurrection-body promises as fully available now; selective engagement with counter-examples like Job and 1 Corinthians 11; and, most seriously, a pastoral-harm concern that this theology can "lay responsibility" for illness at the feet of sick or grieving people, including a specific account in the review of Wommack telling grieving parents their child's death reflected insufficient faith. This is a reviewer's account, not independently re-verified here, but it's a serious enough pastoral-care flag that it should be represented fairly rather than omitted.
Sources: [The True Nature of God](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/2738000-the-true-nature-of-god), [God Wants You Well](https://store.awmi.net/purchase/330), [TGC/Themelios review with cautions](https://www.thegospelcoalition.org/themelios/review/god-wants-you-well-what-the-bible-really-says-about-walking-in-divine-health/)

**F.F. Bosworth** — Early-20th-century evangelist (1877–1958), key figure in the healing-revival movement. Best entry point: **"Christ the Healer"** (1924; revised/expanded edition still in print, 800,000+ copies sold) — roughly half scriptural case-building (Christ's healing ministry, Acts, OT healing promises), half point-by-point rebuttal of objections to healing. A genuine classic that most later healing teachers (including Wommack and Ellis's recommended reading) draw on directly or indirectly. *Caution:* the book's central premise — healing as fully co-equal with forgiveness in the atonement, available now rather than "already/not yet" — is the same live scholarly debate flagged in Section 4; Bosworth represents one of the earliest and most influential statements of the healing-affirming side, not a theologically uncontested consensus.
Sources: [Christ the Healer overview](https://www.cswisdom.com/christ-the-healer-by-f-f-bosworth/), [Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/Christ-Healer-F-Bosworth/dp/0800794575)

**Watchman Nee** — Chinese church leader (1903–1972) imprisoned by the Communist government for his faith and died in a labor camp; widely respected across traditions as a costly-discipleship figure, distinct in tone from the American healing/prosperity-adjacent teachers on this list. Best entry points: **"The Normal Christian Life"** (deep-dive on Romans 6, "reckoning" yourself dead to sin and alive to God, walking by the Spirit rather than soul-power) and **"Sit, Walk, Stand"** (a compact 3-part framework: *sitting* = resting in our finished position in Christ, *walking* = daily conduct flowing from that position, *standing* = spiritual authority/resistance — read by many as a simple front door into "union with Christ" theology). *Caution:* Nee's own teaching isn't seriously disputed, but his legacy is sometimes tangled up with his protégé Witness Lee's later "Local Church" movement, which countercult ministries flagged as controversial in the 1970s–90s over Trinity/"mingling" language and separatist church claims. Worth noting for completeness: CRI (Christian Research Institute) later published a multi-part reassessment walking back much of that earlier criticism — so treat the historical controversy as real but since substantially re-evaluated, and largely about Lee's later movement rather than Nee's own books.
Sources: [Sit, Walk, Stand summary](https://morgantrotter.wordpress.com/2015/06/07/book-summary-of-the-normal-christian-life-by-watchman-nee/), [CRI reassessment of the Local Church controversy](https://www.equip.org/articles/the-local-church-as-movement-and-source-of-controversy/), [CRI: "We Were Wrong" conclusion](https://www.equip.org/articles/the-conclusion-of-the-matter-we-were-wrong-a-reassessment-of-the-local-church-movement-of-watchman-nee-and-witness-lee/)

**Joseph Prince** — Senior pastor of New Creation Church (Singapore), one of the most influential grace/righteousness preachers globally. Best entry point: **"Destined to Reign: The Secret to Effortless Success, Wholeness and Victorious Living"** — argues righteousness is a *received gift*, not an achievement, and that "right believing" (not right behaving) is what produces victorious living, health, and freedom from condemnation. Paul Ellis himself has praised Prince (see "Top 20 Joseph Prince Quotes" on E2R) and treats him as a fellow grace preacher. *Caution:* this is the book that draws the sharpest "cheap grace" critique in this whole research set — a Gospel Coalition Australia review explicitly invokes Bonhoeffer's phrase, arguing Prince's version of grace risks decoupling grace from any real call to discipleship or transformation. Worth flagging clearly on the site as the most theologically contested figure of the group.
Sources: [Destined to Reign overview](https://www.blinkist.com/en/books/destined-to-reign-en), [TGC Australia critical review — "cheap grace" language](https://au.thegospelcoalition.org/article/the-hypergrace-of-joseph-prince-a-review-of-destined-to-reign/), [Ellis on Prince](https://escapetoreality.org/2010/08/24/joseph-prince-preacher-of-grace/)

**E.W. Kenyon** — New England Bible teacher (1867–1948), founder of Bethel Bible Institute, author of 18 books; historically influential as the theological source many trace the modern Word of Faith movement back to (Kenneth Hagin, often called the movement's "father," was heavily shaped by Kenyon's writing — Kenyon is sometimes called the movement's "grandfather"). Best entry point: **"New Creation Realities"** (what you *are* in Christ post-conversion — closely parallel to Ellis's "Ten Words" identity framework) or, for the healing angle specifically, **"Jesus the Healer."** *Caution — this is the most theologically serious flag in this entire document:* Kenyon's "Identification" doctrine (the idea that Christ had to die *spiritually*, take on a sin-nature, and suffer torment in hell before His resurrection, in order to fully identify with fallen humanity) is a distinct and historically controversial claim, separate from the mainstream "penal substitution" atonement most evangelicals hold, and has been sharply criticized as unbiblical/heretical by evangelical apologetics organizations (e.g., the Christian Research Institute's "What's Wrong with the Word Faith Movement?" series). There's also real scholarly disagreement about Kenyon's own intellectual roots — some trace his "positive confession" ideas to the New Thought metaphysical movement, others argue his roots are entirely within the Holiness/Faith Cure/Higher Life movements of his era. This should be flagged plainly if Kenyon is cited on the site — his "what I confess, I possess" material is the piece most likely to need a caution note attached.
Sources: [E.W. Kenyon books list](https://believersportal.com/list-of-books-by-e-w-kenyon/), [CRI: What's Wrong with the Word Faith Movement? (Kenyon)](https://www.equip.org/articles/whats-wrong-with-the-word-faith-movement-part-one/), [New Creation Realities](https://www.ewkenyonbooks.com/catalog/new-creation-realities-e-w-kenyon-9781577700036)

## 6. MISCONCEPTIONS GOLDMINE

**1. "I have to confess every sin, one by one, or I'm not forgiven."**
Grace answer: Forgiveness was secured once, fully, at the cross (Hebrews 9:26, 10:14) — confession in the New Covenant is agreeing with what God has already done, not a transaction that produces forgiveness sin-by-sin. Trying to remember/confess every sin to "stay clean" is functionally impossible and misreads a verse (1 John 1:9) most naturally addressed to people not yet walking with God, not a maintenance formula for believers.
Verses: 1 John 1:9; Hebrews 10:14; Hebrews 9:26
Source: [1 John 1:9 – Something Old, Something New](https://escapetoreality.org/2012/06/08/1-john-1v9/)

**2. "God disciplines/punishes believers with sickness, accidents, or hardship to teach them a lesson."**
Grace answer: Jesus is the exact representation of the Father (Hebrews 1:3) and never once made anyone sick to teach them something — He only ever healed. Sickness and suffering are traced in Scripture to a fallen world and the enemy's work, not to God's hand of correction.
Verses: Hebrews 1:3; Acts 10:38; James 1:17
Source: [The 9 Lies of Religion](https://escapetoreality.org/2015/04/01/the-9-lies-of-religion/), [Does God Scourge Us?](https://escapetoreality.org/2013/06/18/does-god-scourge-us/)

**3. "I'm just a sinner saved by grace."**
Grace answer: Scripture repeatedly calls believers "saints" (Gk. *hagioi*, "holy ones"), never "sinners," after conversion — Paul addresses churches full of struggling people as saints. In Christ you're not a patched-up sinner but a new creation, a completely new kind of person, not an improved old one.
Verses: Ephesians 1:1; Philippians 1:1; 2 Corinthians 5:17
Source: [The Chief of Sinners](https://escapetoreality.org/2015/06/03/chief-of-sinners/)

**4. "God's blessing on my life depends on whether I tithe."**
Grace answer: Every blessing a believer has comes through Christ's finished work, not through a percentage of income (Ephesians 1:3). Malachi 3's "robbing God" language was addressed to Israel under the old covenant's specific terms, not a New Covenant giving formula; Paul never once commands tithing in any of his letters.
Verses: Ephesians 1:3; Malachi 3:8-10 (in context); 2 Corinthians 9:6-7
Source: [Should you Tithe?](https://escapetoreality.org/2016/07/19/should-you-tithe/), [Jesus our Tithe](https://escapetoreality.org/2016/07/21/jesus-our-tithe/)

**5. "The Lord's Prayer means I have to keep asking forgiveness daily to stay in good standing with God."**
Grace answer: "Forgive us our debts" functions as ongoing *fellowship* language (staying in relational step with God and others), not a repeated legal transaction — our forgiving others is presented as evidence that we've already received God's forgiveness, not a condition we must meet first to unlock it.
Verses: Matthew 6:12-15; Colossians 3:13
Source: [Did Jesus Put Price Tags on Forgiveness? (Matthew 6:14-15)](https://escapetoreality.org/2015/08/06/forgiveness-matthew-6v14-15/)

**6. "My prayer wasn't answered / I wasn't healed because of some hidden unconfessed sin."**
Grace answer: The New Covenant basis for answered prayer is the finished work of Christ, not a believer's flawless sin-inventory — this teaching can pile condemnation onto people already suffering and become a faith-killer rather than a faith-builder. Delay is more often attributed to spiritual resistance or a believer's own capacity to receive than to God withholding as punishment.
Verses: John 14:13-14; Matthew 21:22; Colossians 2:15
Source: [Dealing with unanswered prayer](https://escapetoreality.org/2011/03/13/dealing-with-unanswered-prayer/)

**7. "Grace is a license to sin / if you preach grace this strongly, people will just do whatever they want."**
Grace answer: Real grace *trains* people toward godliness rather than away from it (Titus 2:11-12) — those who use grace as an excuse to sin either misunderstand their new identity or aren't actually resting in it. Law produces "dead works" and burnout; grace produces good works that flow from a changed heart.
Verses: Romans 6:1-2; Romans 5:20; Titus 2:11-12
Source: [Is Grace a License to Sin?](https://escapetoreality.org/2012/09/24/is-grace-a-license-to-sin/)

**8. "Repentance means feeling sorry enough and turning from sin through willpower."**
Grace answer: Biblical repentance (*metanoia*) means "change of mind," not a self-generated behavioral U-turn — and it's presented in Scripture as a *response* to encountering God's kindness, not a precondition for receiving it.
Verses: Mark 1:15; Romans 2:4
Source: [3 Reasons Why I Don't Preach on Repentance](https://escapetoreality.org/2011/11/28/3-reasons-why-i-dont-preach-on-repentance/)

**9. "You can lose your salvation if you sin too much or fall away."**
Grace answer: Salvation rests on God's unbreakable promise ("he will," not "we will"), not on a believer's ongoing performance — Scripture nowhere states that a genuine child of God can be "unchilded," even though there are real, painful consequences to straying from a life of trust.
Verses: John 10:28-29; Romans 8:38-39; Philippians 1:6
Source: [Once Saved, Always Saved?](https://escapetoreality.org/2024/09/12/once-saved-always-saved/), [Can You Lose Your Salvation?](https://escapetoreality.org/2018/02/01/can-you-lose-your-salvation/)

**10. "It's healthier/safer to mix a little Law in with grace, just to be balanced."**
Grace answer: Galatians is essentially a book-length warning against exactly this — a "little mixture" doesn't stabilize grace, it undermines it, because the old covenant's "we will" promises and the new covenant's "he will" promises operate on fundamentally incompatible logic.
Verses: Galatians 3:1-3; Galatians 5:4; Hebrews 8:13
Source: [The Old vs the New Covenant](https://escapetoreality.org/2024/02/22/the-old-vs-the-new-covenant/)

**11. "Paul's thorn in the flesh proves God doesn't always want to heal, so I shouldn't expect it either."**
Grace answer: The "thorn" is explicitly named a "messenger of Satan" sent to torment/oppose Paul (harassment/persecution, in context with the list of afflictions in 2 Cor 11:23-29), not a divinely-appointed illness — so it's a weak proof-text for "God withholds healing." *(Flag: this is genuinely disputed among Bible scholars broadly, not settled — see Section 4.)*
Verses: 2 Corinthians 12:7-10; 2 Corinthians 11:23-29
Source: [Andrew Wommack: Paul's Thorn in the Flesh](https://www.awmi.net/browse-teaching/pauls-thorn-in-the-flesh/)

**12. "God is keeping a record of my sins / God is mad at me."**
Grace answer: Love "keeps no record of wrongs" (1 Corinthians 13:5) and 1 John 4:18 says perfected love casts out fear — a God who is simultaneously loving and quietly furious at the same people is a contradiction the cross was specifically meant to resolve (2 Corinthians 5:19, "not counting people's sins against them").
Verses: 1 Corinthians 13:5; 1 John 4:18; 2 Corinthians 5:19
Source: [The 9 Lies of Religion](https://escapetoreality.org/2015/04/01/the-9-lies-of-religion/)

**13. "I relate to God best as His servant — I need to earn my place and prove my usefulness."**
Grace answer: The Bible's dominant relational category for believers is *son/daughter*, not servant — service is meant to flow out of secure belovedness, not be the means of securing it. The prodigal's older brother worked for years without ever enjoying his father's house; sonship, not labor, was always the point.
Verses: Luke 15:11-32; Romans 8:15-17; Galatians 4:6-7
Source: [Son, Servant, or Friend of God?](https://escapetoreality.org/2010/10/04/son-servant-or-friend-of-god/)

**14. "God gave me this trial/sickness to strengthen my character — I just need to endure it as His will."**
Grace answer: Scripture distinguishes between God *permitting* a fallen, enemy-touched world to produce hardship and God *authoring* that hardship — Job's suffering is explicitly attributed to Satan, with God's permission, not God's initiative, and the healing-affirming case adds that access to torment believers changed decisively at the cross.
Verses: Job 2:3-7; Job 1:6-12; Colossians 2:15
Source: [Job's Healing: Did God Really Cause His Suffering?](https://graceforhealing.com/jobs-healing-god-responsible/)

**15. "Taking communion carelessly makes you sick — so if I'm sick, maybe it's God's judgment on me."**
Grace answer (contested — present both sides): the traditional reading ties 1 Corinthians 11:29-30's sickness to the Corinthians' specific relational misconduct at the shared meal (excluding/humiliating poorer believers), not a generalized warning that God zaps casual communion-takers with illness; the healing-camp reading instead ties the sickness to failing to grasp/receive what communion pictures (Christ's healing provision), not to punishment as such. Either way, "God is striking me sick over communion" is a shakier read than either camp's actual argument.
Verses: 1 Corinthians 11:29-30; 1 Corinthians 11:17-22 (context)
Source: [Escape to Reality: 1 Corinthians 11:26-32](https://escapetoreality.org/2011/04/12/1-corinthians-1126-32/)

## Summary of flagged disputed/speculative items (for editorial awareness)

- **1 John 1 addressed to unbelievers, not believers** — Ellis's minority reading; most commentators read 1 John 1 as written to genuine believers.
- **Hebrews 12:6 "scourgeth" reinterpreted as persecution-endurance, not divine discipline** — Ellis's minority/contested reading against the more common view that Hebrews 12 describes real (if loving) divine discipline including hard providence.
- **Healing fully "in the atonement" and available now vs. "already/not yet"** — a genuine, live, unsettled debate across evangelical scholarship generally, not a fringe-vs-mainstream issue.
- **Paul's thorn in the flesh (2 Cor 12:7)** — genuinely unresolved among scholars broadly (persecution vs. eye disease vs. other physical ailment vs. temptation).
- **1 Corinthians 11:29-30 and sickness** — two live, contested readings (relational/disciplinary misconduct vs. failure to appropriate healing).
- **E.W. Kenyon's "Identification" doctrine** (Christ died spiritually / took on a sin-nature / suffered in hell before resurrection) — a historically controversial claim distinct from mainstream penal substitution, criticized as heretical by some evangelical apologetics organizations. Flag clearly if using Kenyon material.
- **Andrew Wommack pastoral-harm critique** — sourced from a single third-party review (Themelios/TGC); represented here as the reviewer's account, not independently re-verified.
- **Joseph Prince "cheap grace" critique** — a substantive, named theological critique (invoking Bonhoeffer) from a mainstream evangelical review outlet; Prince is the most contested figure in this research set.
